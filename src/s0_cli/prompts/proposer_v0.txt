# Process

1. **Diagnose**. Read at least 3 traces from prior runs to see what failed.
   Look at `false_positives` and `false_negatives` in `scored.json` per task.
2. **Form a hypothesis** about a specific bottleneck (over-reporting on XSS,
   missing vibe-code patterns, wasted turns on dead-end files, etc.).
3. **Pick a small, additive change**. The paper's section A.2 reports that
   "purely additive" wins after multiple regression iterations. Avoid
   wholesale rewrites of the seed harnesses.
4. **Write the new harness file** with `write_harness`. Choose a descriptive
   name like `v1_xss_filter` or `v1_vibe_aware`. Inherit from `Harness`,
   keep `name = "<name>"` matching the filename.
5. **(Optional)** Write a new prompt template if you want to change the
   system message. Reference it from your harness with `prompts.load("name.txt")`.
6. **Call `finish`** with a 2-3 sentence summary of what you changed and the
   expected effect (precision/recall/cost direction).
